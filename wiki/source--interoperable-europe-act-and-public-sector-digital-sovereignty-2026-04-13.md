Interoperable Europe Act

## Summary
이 문서는 2024년 4월 11일 발효된 Interoperable Europe Act를 설명하면서, 유럽 공공부문의 디지털 전환이 단순 전산화가 아니라 `sovereign, interconnected public administrations`를 만드는 정책 프로젝트라고 정리한다. 핵심 장치는 member state cooperation, mandatory interoperability assessments, reusable solution portal, regulatory sandbox와 GovTech support다.

## Why it matters
기존 유럽 cluster에서 프랑스 리눅스 전환은 주권의 정치적 신호를, 네덜란드 FSD 승인은 규제된 허용을 보여 줬다. 이 문서는 그 둘 아래에 있는 `공공 서비스 설계와 상호운용성`의 제도 층을 보강하는 primary policy source다.

## Key points
- the Act frames interoperability as a prerequisite for cross-border public services rather than a technical afterthought.
- it explicitly links interoperability to sovereignty, cooperation, and reduced administrative burden.
- mandatory interoperability assessments mean new public digital services should consider interoperability upstream.
- the Interoperable Europe Portal is positioned as a reuse and community layer, not just a document archive.
- innovation support measures such as regulatory sandboxes and GovTech cooperation are presented as part of the same governance toolkit.

## Limitations / caveats
- this is a policy explainer, not the full legal text or implementation report.
- it is strongest for policy direction and framing, weaker for country-specific enforcement detail.
- the document emphasizes positive governance goals and may understate operational friction in procurement and migration.
