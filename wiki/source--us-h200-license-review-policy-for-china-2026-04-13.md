## Title
Department of Commerce Revises License Review Policy for Semiconductors Exported to China

- `raw/web-snapshots/Department of Commerce Revises License Review Policy for Semiconductors Exported to China.md`

## Type
domain-web-article

## Summary
이 발표는 Nvidia H200, AMD MI325X 같은 advanced chip의 대중국 수출을 `certain security requirements` 아래 case-by-case license review로 허용할 수 있다고 밝힌다. 핵심은 blanket prohibition이나 blanket permission이 아니라, global capacity impact, purchaser compliance, U.S.-based third-party testing을 조건으로 한 controlled access model이다.

## Why it matters
이 문서는 compute control이 `누가 막는가`만이 아니라 `어떤 조건에서 예외적으로 통과시키는가`의 문제이기도 하다는 점을 보여 준다. 장기 alliance와 sovereign procurement가 내부 access를 정한다면, export licensing은 국경을 넘는 access gate를 세부 조건으로 조정한다.

## Key points
- the rule allows case-by-case review for H200, MI325X, and similar chips bound for China.
- applicants must show exports do not materially reduce capacity available to U.S. customers.
- Chinese purchasers must adopt compliance procedures and screening.
- independent third-party testing in the U.S. is framed as part of the approval condition.
- the change presents export control as a calibrated industrial-policy instrument rather than a binary ban.

## Limitations / caveats
- this source is a BIS press release summary, not the full Federal Register text.
- case-by-case review means actual access still depends on later licensing practice.
- the rule speaks to high-end chip exports, not the entire AI infrastructure stack.
