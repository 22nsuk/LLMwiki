## Summary
이 글은 coffee water chemistry를 `pH`보다 `alkalinity`와 `beverage ratio`의 상호작용으로 설명한다. 핵심은 espresso와 filter가 같은 물을 써도 산미 체감과 buffering capacity가 다르며, 따라서 물 레시피는 추출 방식별로 달라져야 한다는 점이다. practical implication은 water mineral issue를 단순 taste preference가 아니라 `buffering budget` 문제로 다시 읽게 만든다.

## Why it matters
현재 coffee chemistry corpus에는 dissolved cation과 hot/cold brew chemistry anchor가 있지만, 실제 현장에서 `왜 espresso와 filter에 같은 물 standard를 그대로 쓰기 어려운가`를 설명하는 bridge source는 없었다. 이 글은 theory-to-practice 연결면을 메워 준다.

## Key points
- alkalinity is presented as more operationally important than pH for extraction-related acidity.
- beverage ratio changes how much buffering capacity is available relative to the amount of coffee acids extracted.
- espresso can tolerate higher alkalinity than filter before acidity is softened to the same extent.
- scaling filter-water recommendations mechanically into espresso can create scale and boiler problems.
- water recipes should therefore balance sensory goals with technical constraints such as scale formation.

## Limitations / caveats
- this is a practical explanatory article, not a controlled experiment.
- it is strongest for operational intuition and recipe logic, weaker for universally validated target values.
- the discussion simplifies some interactions between hardness, alkalinity, scale risk, and extraction outcome.
