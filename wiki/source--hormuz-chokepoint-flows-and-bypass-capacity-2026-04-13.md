## Summary
IEA의 `Strait of Hormuz - About`와 EIA의 `World Oil Transit Chokepoints`는 호르무즈 해협이 단순 전쟁 headline이 아니라, 2025년 기준 세계 seaborne oil trade와 LNG trade의 핵심 병목이라는 점을 수치로 보여 준다. 두 자료를 같이 보면 호르무즈를 통과하는 원유·제품·LNG 규모, 아시아 의존도, Saudi/UAE bypass pipeline의 제한된 여력, prolonged disruption이 곧바로 world oil and gas market shock로 번지는 구조가 더 선명해진다.

## Why it matters
기존 middle-east shipping synthesis는 외교 교착, 봉쇄 수사, 유가 재가격, 잔존 위협에 강했지만, `병목 자체의 구조적 규모`와 `bypass capacity의 한계`를 보여 주는 official background anchor는 없었다. 이 source는 current crisis reading을 더 구조적인 chokepoint geography 위에 올려 준다.

## Key points
- EIA estimates around 20.9 million barrels per day of oil moved through Hormuz in 1H25.
- IEA emphasizes that roughly a quarter of global seaborne oil trade and around one-fifth of global LNG trade depend on Hormuz.
- most crude and LNG moving through the strait is destined for Asian markets, with China, India, Japan, and Korea especially exposed.
- bypass routes in Saudi Arabia and the UAE exist but can reroute only a limited portion of flows.
- prolonged disruption would be expected to create both price spikes and physical shortages because replacement capacity is limited.

## Limitations / caveats
- these are structural background explainers rather than conflict-specific operational updates.
- flow estimates differ slightly by source and methodology, so the safe reuse point is the order of magnitude and structural dependence.
- these documents do not by themselves tell us whether a short-lived military flare-up will actually shut the strait.
