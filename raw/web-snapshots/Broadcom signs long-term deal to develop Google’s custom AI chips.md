April 6 (Reuters) - Broadcom [(AVGO.O), opens new tab](https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/AVGO.O) said on Monday it has signed a long-term agreement with Google [(GOOGL.O), opens new tab](https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/GOOGL.O) to develop and supply future generations of custom artificial intelligence chips and other components for the ‌company's next-generation AI racks through 2031.

The chip firm also signed a deal with Anthropic to provide the AI startup access to about 3.5 gigawatts of AI computing capacity drawing on Google's AI processors, starting in 2027.

Financial terms of the ⁠deals were not disclosed.

Shares of Broadcom rose about 3% in extended trading.

Demand for custom chips such as Google's tensor processing units (TPUs), used for AI workloads, has [surged](https://www.reuters.com/business/meta-talks-spend-billions-googles-chips-information-reports-2025-11-25/) in recent years as businesses seek alternatives to Nvidia's pricey graphics processors.

Reuters [reported](https://www.reuters.com/business/google-works-erode-nvidias-software-advantage-with-metas-help-2025-12-17/) in December that Google was pushing to make its TPUs a viable alternative to Nvidia's market-leading GPUs. TPU sales have become a crucial growth engine ‌of ⁠Google's cloud revenue as it seeks to prove to investors that its AI investments are generating returns.

Anthropic said on Monday that the new deal builds on the company's commitment to invest $50 billion in ⁠strengthening U.S. computing infrastructure.

Demand for its AI model Claude has accelerated in 2026, with the startup's run-rate revenue now surpassing $30 billion, up ⁠from about $9 billion at the end of 2025, it said.

Anthropic said it trains and runs Claude on a range ⁠of AI hardware, including Amazon Web Services' Trainium, Google TPUs, and Nvidia GPUs.

Amazon [(AMZN.O), opens new tab](https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/AMZN.O) remains Anthropic's primary cloud provider and training partner.
